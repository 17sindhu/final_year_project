Abstract

Online product reviews from Karnataka buyers are routinely written in Romanized Kannada — Kannada words typed in English letters rather than Unicode script. There is no agreed spelling convention and the same word appears in 4–5 surface forms depending on the writer.

This paper presents a complete pipeline: a 10,000-review annotated dataset (Cohen kappa = 0.89), a hand-built phonetic normalization dictionary, and a controlled comparison of 15 model configurations across ML, DL, and Transformer families. mBERT achieves 78.00% accuracy while SVM reaches 77.68% at far lower cost.

Keywords: Sentiment Analysis • Romanized Kannada • Code-Mixed NLP • mBERT • BiLSTM • TF-IDF • Phonetic Normalization

Why It Matters

Spelling variation was the dominant challenge. The normalization dictionary improved ML accuracy by 3–5 percentage points. Logistic Regression improved by +5.25 pp and Naive Bayes by +4.23 pp over the FIRE 2021 baseline.

Key Findings

mBERT leads at 78.00%, but the 0.32 pp gap over SVM is narrow given the significant difference in computational cost.

Preprocessing quality contributes as much to accuracy as model architecture — the normalization dictionary delivered 3–5 pp gains across all families.

GRU underperformed at 43.70% due to training instability on this corpus size.
